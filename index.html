What was your first impression of the interactive experience?

The first thing that caught my attention was the illustrated beach scene with an airplane at dawn. Above it, there was a line of text: “Resize your browser to make the sunrise.” As I adjusted the browser width, the sun gradually rose in response to my movement. Afterwards, the “Start” button appeared.

02

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions!

In the first two minutes, I started by dragging the wheel to move back and forth between different scenes, then used my mouse to explore the animation more closely. As I became more engaged, I clicked and held on the animation to activate immersive audio, and then opened the three dots to read the poetry. After that, I searched for hidden elements within the animation and clicked on them to trigger movement and sound.

03

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The experience I spent the most time on was probably dragging my mouse around the animation, as it was very detailed with many interesting elements that you need to look closely to fully notice and appreciate. At the same time, when I clicked and held, the background music would stop, allowing more diverse and immersive sounds to emerge.

04

What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction?

The most common action in my two-minute interaction was dragging the clock wheel to navigate between different points in time. I repeatedly dragged it back and forth to revisit animations I found interesting, and this action continued consistently throughout the first two minutes and even until the end of the experience.

05

What is your impression of the primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression is that the primary goal of the interactive experience is to promote Richard Mille’s RM 07-01 Coloured Ceramics watch in a much more subtle and emotional way. While the ultimate aim is still to increase awareness and sales, Richard Mille uses this immersive “Endless Summer” journey to engage users through exploration, sound, and storytelling. This approach connects the luxurious brand with a chic lifestyle and builds a deeper emotional connection with potential customers, rather than relying on conventional advertising.

06

How does the interactive experience communicate its primary goal?

In my opinion, the interactive experience communicates its primary goal mainly through visuals and sound. Visually, it is structured around three different times of the day, and throughout these moments, it presents animations of familiar daily activities that are romanticised through a consistent colour palette. The use of saturated, playful colours strongly reflects the feeling of summer and helps create a cohesive mood across the experience.

In terms of sound, there is a background track that helps maintain this consistency of mood. However, when the user clicks and holds, additional sounds are revealed. These sounds are very familiar and relatable, similar to what you would hear in real life, which allows me to imagine myself within the scene just by listening.

07

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

In my opinion, this experience should be explored slowly and gradually, taking time to fully engage with all the features it offers. It is not just about watching the animation, but also experiencing the audio, reading the poetry, and discovering the hidden interactive elements. I think it should be revisited around three or four times to truly appreciate it, as there are many small details that you may only notice with each repeated viewing.

08

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

In Endless Summer, there is a wheel used to navigate between the three phases of a summer day. This clock-like wheel requires the user to move through each time segment in order to reach the next phase, which suggests that the experience is not meant to be rushed but rather explored over time.

Additionally, the need to drag the mouse or tilt the device to fully view the animation in each scene also communicates that the experience should be engaged with slowly and actively, encouraging users to take their time to discover different elements.

09

What other media forms does the experience reference?

Based on my observation, the experience references several media forms, including video games, animated films, digital illustration, and poetry.

10

What do these references communicate about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These references suggest that I should actively explore the environment rather than passively observe it. Similar to video games, the experience encourages curiosity and experimentation, prompting me to interact with each scene, click or hold on elements, and discover hidden details.

11

What do these references communicate about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Like animated films—especially illustration-based animations, the scenes are constructed in a way that blends realism with subtle exaggeration. Everyday environments feel believable through details like lighting, texture, and small movements, but at the same time, elements such as colour, composition, and pacing are slightly heightened. This does not just show reality as it is but reshapes it, making moments feel more intentional and emotional. Because of this, I feel encouraged to slow down and spend more time with each scene, rather than just moving through it quickly.

Poetry is also incorporated into the experience, functioning almost like a quiet narrative voice. It does not fully explain what I am seeing, but instead adds another layer of meaning, allowing me to interpret the scene more personally. As a result, I feel more emotionally connected and begin to view the experience in a more romantic and reflective way.

12

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction for me is searching for the hidden objects. In some scenes, I spent time clicking everywhere trying to find them, but it seems that this feature is only included in certain scenes and not others. If it is presented as part of the interaction in the instructions, I think it should be incorporated more consistently throughout the experience.

13

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

In my opinion, the most satisfying part for me was noticing the small sounds that align closely with the illustrations. For example, in the tennis scene, there is a subtle tennis grunt when the players hit the ball, the sound of a plane passing by, and even the soft purring of a cat, even though it is only placed in a small corner of the animation. These details made me feel that the experience was very carefully considered and thoughtfully curated, and it allowed me to become fully immersed in the scene.
