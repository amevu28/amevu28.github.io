01

The first thing that caught my attention was the beautiful illustration scene of a beach with an airplane during dawn, above which was the text 'Resize your browser to make the sunrise'’, as I made the browser narrower horizontally,
the sun gradually rose following my movement. Then, the “Start” button appears.

02

In the first two minutes, I started by dragging the wheel to move back and forth between different scenes, then used my mouse to explore the animation more closely. As I became more engaged, I clicked and held on the animation to activate immersive audio, and then opened the three dots to read the poetry. After that, I searched for hidden elements within the animation and clicked on them to trigger movement and sound.

03

The experience I spent the most time on was probably dragging my mouse around the animation, as it was very detailed with many interesting elements that you need to look closely to fully notice and appreciate. At the same time, when I clicked and held, the background music would stop, allowing more diverse and immersive sounds to emerge.

04

The most common action in my two-minute interaction was dragging the clock wheel to navigate between different points in time. I repeatedly dragged it back and forth to revisit animations I found interesting, and this action continued consistently throughout the first two minutes and even until the end of the experience.

05

My impression is that the primary goal of the interactive experience is to promote Richard Mille’s RM 07-01 Coloured Ceramics watch in a much more subtle and emotional way. While the ultimate aim is still to increase awareness and sales, Richard Mille uses this immersive “Endless Summer” journey to engage users through exploration, sound, and storytelling. This approach connects the luxurious brand with a chic lifestyle and builds a deeper emotional connection with potential customers, rather than relying on conventional advertising.
